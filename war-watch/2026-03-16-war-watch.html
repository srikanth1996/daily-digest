Srikanth — War Watch

🔴 March 16, 2026 · 9:30 AM IST

Good morning. This is your War Watch catchup for March 16, 2026.

Day eighteen. A fire broke out in the vicinity of Dubai International Airport following Iranian drone strikes, temporarily halting flight operations at one of the world's busiest aviation hubs. Separately, an Iranian missile fragment struck the United States Consul residence building in Israel, injuring two American diplomatic staff. Pope Leo, speaking from the Vatican, denounced the conflict's atrocious violence and called on all parties to immediately pursue a ceasefire and humanitarian dialogue.

On the military front, American forces launched their most concentrated single-day strike package against Iran's Kharg Island oil export terminal. Satellite imagery shows severe damage to loading infrastructure. Iran retaliated with ballistic missile salvos targeting American naval vessels in the Gulf of Oman, with the USS Abraham Lincoln carrier group intercepting multiple incoming projectiles. Israel continued attacking Hezbollah command infrastructure in Baalbek, eastern Lebanon.

Diplomatically, the UN Security Council convened an emergency session at India's request. New Delhi submitted a formal resolution calling for a forty-eight-hour humanitarian pause to allow civilian evacuations. Russia and China co-sponsored the resolution. The United States vetoed it, but the diplomatic manoeuvre elevated India's standing as a neutral peace broker in the eyes of the Global South.

For India, the Dubai airport closure is causing severe disruptions. Over two hundred thousand Indians transit through Dubai monthly. Flight diversions to Muscat and Colombo are adding hours to journeys. Indian Oil Corporation confirmed it has secured alternative crude supply contracts with the US and Brazil covering the next sixty days, providing some relief to the LPG supply chain.

Watch for India's ceasefire diplomacy at the UN to gather momentum this week.
