Srikanth — War Watch

🔴 March 15, 2026 · 9:30 AM IST

Good morning. This is your War Watch catchup for March 15, 2026.

Day seventeen. The United States Embassy in Baghdad sustained the most intense drone attack since the war began. A coordinated swarm of Iranian-backed drones struck the compound in the Green Zone overnight, injuring multiple personnel. The Pentagon called it a direct escalation by Tehran-aligned militia groups and warned of severe consequences.

On the military front, Iran struck the UAE port of Fujairah, a critical oil transit hub that processes shipments bypassing the Strait of Hormuz. The attack caused fires at two storage terminals and halted operations for several hours. Dubai International Airport temporarily suspended flights after missile threats were tracked on radar, creating chaos for millions of transit passengers. Israel launched fresh strikes on Beirut's southern suburbs following a Hezbollah rocket barrage that killed twelve people along Lebanon's coastline.

Diplomatically, Secretary of State Rubio called the Baghdad embassy attack a red line and summoned Iran's UN representative for an emergency meeting. Britain confirmed it transferred two military bases to American forces specifically to help protect the eight to nine million Indians and South Asians residing in Gulf countries. India welcomed this, while urging all parties toward de-escalation.

For India, the Fujairah attack is a direct blow. The port processes a significant share of India's petroleum imports. Oil companies have activated emergency routing via the Cape of Good Hope, adding twenty days to delivery times. Aviation fuel costs surged ten percent overnight. Indian community associations in Dubai are urging calm as residents report anxiety over the conflict intensifying near their homes.

Watch for US military response to the Baghdad embassy attack over the next forty-eight hours.
