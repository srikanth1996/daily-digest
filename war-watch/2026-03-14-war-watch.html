War Watch — India's Daily Brief: March 14, 2026

Srikanth — War Watch

🔴 March 14, 2026 · 9:30 AM IST

Good morning. This is your War Watch catchup for March 14, 2026.

Day sixteen of the US-Iran war. Mojtaba Khamenei formally assumed power as Iran's Supreme Leader in a televised ceremony in Tehran, flanked by senior Revolutionary Guard commanders. In his inaugural address, he announced three formal conditions for ending the war: full withdrawal of American forces from the region, a permanent end to Israeli strikes on Iranian soil, and immediate lifting of all economic sanctions.

On the military front, the United States continued precision strikes on Iranian missile production facilities in Isfahan and Tabriz overnight. Israel launched new air raids over southern Lebanon after Hezbollah resumed rocket fire into northern Israel. The UAE formally lodged a protest at the United Nations, disclosing that over eighteen hundred Iranian drones and missiles have been fired at Gulf states since February twenty-eighth.

Diplomatically, the US State Department rejected Khamenei's three conditions as non-starters, but acknowledged receipt. European Union foreign ministers convened an emergency session in Brussels calling for an immediate ceasefire. India's External Affairs Minister Jaishankar held bilateral calls with both the UAE and Saudi Arabia foreign ministers, expressing India's deep concern over the Hormuz blockade and securing assurances of priority crude supplies.

For India, the situation is worsening. Petrol rationing has formally begun in parts of Gujarat. Airlines are announcing fuel surcharges on domestic routes. The Centre is fast-tracking alternative crude procurement from the US, Angola, and Brazil to replace Iranian and Gulf supplies. Indian nationals in Iran are being advised to prepare for possible evacuation.

Watch for the UN Security Council emergency session and India's emergency energy summit.
