Good morning. I'm Srikanth, and this is your War Watch bulletin for March 19, 2026.

Iran escalated overnight, striking Gulf energy infrastructure in a move that sent crude oil surging to one hundred and fifteen dollars per barrel — the highest since the conflict began. Qatar's Ras Laffan industrial city, one of the world's largest LNG hubs, reported extensive damage after Iranian missile strikes hit the complex. The global energy market is now on full alert.

On the military front, Israel confirmed the killing of Iranian Intelligence Minister Esmail Khatib in a targeted strike earlier this week. China condemned the elimination of Iranian leadership figures as unacceptable, warning of regional destabilisation. Meanwhile, US defence officials are weighing significant military reinforcements as the conflict enters what analysts are calling a dangerous new phase. Drones were detected over a US base where Secretary of State Rubio and Defence Secretary Hegseth are stationed, raising the threat level further.

On the diplomatic front, President Trump confirmed that Israel acted independently in striking Iran's gas fields, but warned Tehran directly against targeting Qatar. Russia-Ukraine peace negotiations have been suspended, with both sides citing the Iran conflict as a disruptive factor in global security talks.

For India, the pain is immediate. Brent crude at one hundred and fifteen dollars per barrel means higher petrol, diesel, and LPG costs within days. Twenty-two India-bound ships remain on the Hormuz evacuation watch-list, though the tanker Jag Laadki reached Mundra Port safely today. India's planned free trade agreement talks with Gulf nations are now stalled indefinitely.

Watch tomorrow for any Iranian counter-strike on Qatar and whether India activates emergency crude reserves.
